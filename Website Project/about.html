More Info

June 6th 1946, in New York, the NBL (National Basketball League) and the BAA, (Basketball Association of America) combined to make the NBA (National Basketball Association). When the NBA first started, there were only 17 teams. Presently, there are now 30 teams, with 15 teams per conference. The 2 conferences are the Western Conference and the Eastern Conference.

Origin of Basketball

In 1891, a Canadian man named James Naismith was teaching his students. He found that the students he was teaching had too much energy, and that he needed to find a way to burn off their excess energy. James then brought two peach baskets and a soccer ball for his equipment and nailed both baskets on opposite sides, 10 feet high. James Naismith decided to call the game he made "Basketball". Later on as the sport developed, different rules were added to the game like dribbling. Different modifications were also made, such as adding backboards, replacing the baskets with nets, and introducing a new type of ball to be used for basketball.
